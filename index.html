By tracking top teams, player development, peak performance ages, and playing styles, it tells a data-driven story, blending analysis and visualization to reveal the hidden patterns in the beautiful game.

Insights

Excel Project

An Excel project analyzed student performance in the 2023 KCSE exam across 19 schools in a sub-county, comparing results with 2022 and 2021. The analysis covered grade distribution, gender performance, school rankings, deviation indices, and subject performance, providing key insights for educational improvements.

Dashboard Article
Insights

13% improvement in students scoring C+ and above in 2023, reflecting a positive trend in higher-grade achievement.

50% increase in students scoring grade E in 2023 compared to 2022, highlighting an area needing targeted support.

Overall, 15% of students attained C+ or higher, with girls at 17% and boys at 13%, showing a slight performance advantage for girls.

Top-performing subjects included Computer Studies (8.51), Home Science (8.32), and Kiswahili (6.54), while Chemistry (2.57), Mathematics (2.55), and Agriculture (2.49) scored the lowest.

Power BI Project

A Power BI project analyzing the performance of 242 students in series exams comprising 14 subjects in a six-streamed school. The analysis focused on comparing results from July and October, examining performance by subject, stream, and grade to predict KCSE outcomes and identify improvement areas.

Dashboard Article
Insights

The school achieved a mean score of 6.65 in the October Series, falling slightly below the target mean of 7.29, indicating room for improvement before KCSE.

Students scoring quality grades C+ or higher increased by 34% in October Series, with 50% achieving this benchmark compared to only 16% in the July Series.

An impressive 99% of students showed improvement in their grades and mean points, while only 1% maintained their July Series results, highlighting significant academic progress overall.

Only 64% of subjects met their set targets.
